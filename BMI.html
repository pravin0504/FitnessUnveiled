What Is BMI ????

BMI stands for Body Mass Index. It is a numerical value of a person's weight in relation to their height. BMI is a commonly used tool to categorize individuals into different weight categories, which can be helpful in assessing whether a person is underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese.

To calculate BMI, you can use the following formula:

BMI = (weight in kilograms) / (height in meters)^2 Here's how the BMI categories are typically defined for adults:

Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
Normal weight: BMI between 18.5 and 24.9
Overweight: BMI between 25 and 29.9
Obesity (Class I): BMI between 30 and 34.9
Obesity (Class II): BMI between 35 and 39.9
Obesity (Class III): BMI 40 or greater
