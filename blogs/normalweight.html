Your BMI falls within the normal weight range (between 18.5 and 24.9)

It suggests that you have a healthy weight in relation to your height.

However, it's important to remember that BMI is just one measure of health, and it doesn't take into account other factors such as muscle mass, body composition, or overall fitness.

Here are some general tips for maintaining and promoting good health when you have a normal BMI:

Healthy Eating: Continue to maintain a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats. Eating a variety of nutrient-dense foods is essential for overall well-being.

Regular Exercise: Engage in regular physical activity to stay fit and maintain good cardiovascular health. Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic exercise per week, along with strength training exercises at least two days a week.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day to stay properly hydrated. Water is essential for various bodily functions.

Monitor Health: Even if your BMI is in the normal range, it's a good idea to have regular check-ups with your healthcare provider. They can assess other aspects of your health, such as blood pressure, cholesterol levels, and overall well-being.

Manage Stress: Find healthy ways to manage stress, as chronic stress can have a negative impact on your health. Techniques like meditation, deep breathing, and exercise can help reduce stress.

Adequate Sleep: Ensure you get enough quality sleep each night. Most adults need 7-9 hours of sleep to function optimally.

Limit Alcohol and Avoid Smoking: If you drink alcohol, do so in moderation. Avoid smoking or seek assistance to quit if you are a smoker.

Mental Health: Pay attention to your mental health. Seek support or professional help if you are dealing with stress, anxiety, or depression.

Stay Informed: Stay informed about health-related issues and make informed decisions about your health.

Remember that individual health needs can vary, so it's important to consult with a healthcare professional who can provide personalized advice based on your specific circumstances. Additionally, maintaining a healthy lifestyle and preventing health problems is not solely about weight but encompasses various aspects of physical and mental well-being.
